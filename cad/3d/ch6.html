2. Preset Views

Sets the 3D view while in the orbit command

1.   Click with the right mouse button while in the 3D Orbit command

2.   Choose Preset Views from the pop-up menu.

3.   Choose one of the following standard 3D views.

3. Free Orbit

1.   Choose View, Orbit, Free Orbit

The 3D Orbit Arcball appears.

2.   Click on one the 3D Orbit arcball location to move the display of

your object(s).

 

Outside the Arcball-Moves View about an axis that

extends through the center (acts like twist)

 

Inside one of the small circles to the left/right-Rotates

around the "Y" axis through the center

 

Inside the Arcball

Allows movement in nay direction

 

Inside one of the small circle to the top/bottom

Rotates around the "X" axis through the center.

 

4. Continuous Orbit

 

1.   Choose View, Orbit, Continuous Orbit

2.   Click and drag to define the direction and speed of a continuous

orbit for your object(s)

3.   Press ESC on the keyboard to stop the orbit.

 

5. Other Navigation Modes

1.   Click  with the right mouse button while in the 3D Orbit command.

2.   Choose Other Navigation Modes from the pop-up menu.

3.   Choose one of the following modes.

 

Adjust Distance(4) Simulates moving the camera closer to the object or farther away.

 

Swivel (5) Change the cursor to an arched arrow and simulates the effect of swiveling the camera.

 

Walk (6) Changes the cursor to a plus sign and enables you to " walk through" a model at a fixed

height above the xy plane, by dynamically controlling the location and target of the camera.

 

Fly (7) Changes the cursor to a plus sign and enable you to "fly through" a model without being

restricted to a fixed height above the XY plane.

 

Zoom (8) Change the cursor to a magnifying glass with plus (+) and minus (-) sign and simulates

moving the camera closer to an object or farther away. Works like the Adjust Distance option.
