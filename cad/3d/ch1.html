1.   Choose  Start from the Windows program manager.

Workspaces

AutoCAD workspace are sets of menus, toolbar and dockable windows (such as the

Properties palette, DesignCenter, and the Tool palettes window) that are grouped and organized so

that you can work in a custom, task-oriented drawing environment.

 

1.   Click   the Workspaces Switching icon.

2.   Click   3D Modeling and OK.

3.Named Views

1.   Choose View, Named Views...

2.   Click the plus    (+)sign beside Preset Views.

3.   Click NE Isometric, SetCurrent, Apply, and OK.

TIP:

You can also choose View, 3D Views, and any of the preset 3D views.

4. Steering Wheel

Steering Wheel are menus that track the cursor over the drawing

window, and provide access to 2D and 3D navigation tools from a

single interface.

Steering Wheels, or "wheels", are divided into wedges: each

wedge contains a single navigation tool. You can start a

navigation tool by clicking a wedge or by clicking and dragging the

cursor over a wedge..

5. VPOINT Command (Tripod)

Display a compass and tripod for defined a view rotation. the

compass represents a two dimensional globe.

 

1.   Choose     View, 3D Views, point

       or

2.   Type         VPOINT at the command prompt. command: vpoint

Rotate/<Viewpoint><-0.614,-0.614,0.500>: (enter)

3.   Click        a point on the compass to define the viewing angle

 

 

Point in the center of the compass is the north pole.

 

6. VPOINT Command (Rotate)

Enters a rotation angle at the viewpoint prompt.

 

1.   Type VPOINT at the command prompt. Command:vpoint

Rotate/<Viewpoint><-0.614,-0.614,0.500>:R (enter)

Enter angle in XY plane from X axis <255>:225(enter)

Enter angle from XY plane <30>: 15 (enter)
